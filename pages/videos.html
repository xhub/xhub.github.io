Sliding Mode Controller on an electro-pneumatic benchmark in Nantes

Implicit and explicit discrete-time twisting controllers from Olivier Huber on Vimeo.

This short video illustrates the difference between two implementations of the twisting controller on an academic benchmark.

The control problem is to make the mass coupling the two pistons follow a sinusoidal trajectory. The left piston is the on we control, whereas the right one is used to impress a perturbation force of 1000N in magnitude, switching between positive and negative sign. This information is not available to the control loop.

The first controller is obtained with an explicit discretization and the chattering, due to this bad choice of discretization method, produces a lot of noise. The latter is produced by the pneumatic-powered distributors.

In the second part of the video, we show how the implicitly discretized control law do not exhibit the same chattering behaviour as the explicit one. As a result, the performance is also greatly improved, as well as the wear on the actuator.

This experiments confirm the superiority of the implicit discretization as shown by the theoretical analysis in the two theoretical papers Acary & Brogliato 2010 and Acary, Brogliato & Orlov 2012.
